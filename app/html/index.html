Basic information

To begin, we need to know how old you are now and at what age you plan to retire, so we can calculate how long you’ll have to save.

While the current average retirement age among retirees is about 60 years old, that’s expected to increase for younger generations. Americans born in 1960 or later can access partial Social Security benefits at age 62 and full benefits at age 67. Some experts recommend holding off on collecting until age 70 because Americans are living longer, meaning your savings need to last longer.

If you’re not sure what to choose, consider going with the default age of 67.

Income

How much money you make will determine how much you’ll need to maintain your standard of living in retirement, as well as how much of your income you should contribute to a 401(k) or other savings plan.

Some experts advise you to use a conservative estimate of 5-6% since it’s better to end up with too much money than too little. For simplicity, we’ll start you off at 6%. If you want to be more conservative or more optimistic, you can adjust this number below.

How much you need

Now comes the tricky part: figuring out how much you'll need to save to live comfortably in retirement. Generally, experts say you'll need 70 to 80% of your income each year to maintain your standard of living, but that isn't the case for everyone.

Lifestyle factors and preferences will affect how much you spend during your retirement. If you're hoping to live a more active lifestyle and travel often, you should add six percentage points to your annual budget.

We'll start you off with an annual spending goal determined by the income you entered above. You can make adjustments based on the expectations you have for your post-work years.
